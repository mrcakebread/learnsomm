Roast Levels
Light RoastSpecialty▼
FlavorBright, floral, acidic
NotesCitrus, berry, jasmine, honey
Best forPour-over or drip — methods that highlight clarity
Pairs withFruit pastries, yogurt, light breakfast fare
Heads upThe "sour" taste some people associate with light roast is usually under-extraction, not the roast itself — a slightly longer brew time helps.
Medium RoastStart Here▼
FlavorBalanced, sweet, nutty
NotesMilk chocolate, toasted nut, caramel
Best forDrip coffee, French press — the most versatile roast
Pairs withButtered toast, banana bread, breakfast pastries
Best forThe "classic" coffee shop default — a safe order if you're unsure
Dark RoastBold▼
FlavorBold, intense, smoky
NotesDark chocolate, molasses, baking spice
Best forFrench press, cold brew — stands up to ice and milk
Pairs withDark chocolate desserts, smoked meats
Heads upDark roasts actually have slightly less caffeine by volume than light roasts — the roasting process burns off some caffeine.
Espresso Roast▼
FlavorIntense, concentrated, syrupy
NotesBrown sugar, baker's chocolate, charred wood
Best forEspresso machines — high pressure extraction needs this roast's oils
Pairs withBiscotti, tiramisu, dark chocolate
Heads up"Espresso roast" describes the roast profile, not a bean type — any bean can be roasted for espresso.
Essential Coffee Variations & Cocktails
Espresso Martini▼
MixEspresso, vodka, coffee liqueur
StyleEnergizing · Rich · Foamy
Order tipThe foam on top comes from shaking hard with ice — a sign of a well-made one is a thick, persistent crema-like layer.
Irish Coffee▼
MixHot coffee, Irish whiskey, sugar, cream float
StyleWarming · Indulgent · Balanced
Order tipThe cream should be lightly whipped and floated, not stirred in — it should sit on top as you drink through it.
Cold Brew Tonic▼
MixCold brew, tonic water, ice, citrus garnish
StyleRefreshing · Effervescent · Bright
Order tipA modern café favorite — the bitterness of tonic plays surprisingly well with the low-acid sweetness of cold brew.
Affogato▼
MixEspresso shot poured over vanilla ice cream
StyleDessert · Creamy · Intense
Order tipPour the espresso right at the table for the best contrast between hot and cold — don't let it sit and melt first.
White Russian▼
MixVodka, coffee liqueur, cream
StyleSweet · Decadent · Smooth
Order tipTechnically doesn't contain coffee itself — the coffee flavor comes entirely from the liqueur (e.g. Kahlúa).
