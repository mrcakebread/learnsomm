Eight ways to render the same wine country

One stylised region, eight presentation modes. This is the toolkit I'd draw from for your AVA maps — pick a mode below, then hit the controls to animate it. The geometry here is illustrative, not survey-accurate; on your real maps these techniques sit on the hand-traced AVA polygons.

Explore click or hover a sub-region
Hover or tap a sub-region to read its profile. In Choropleth mode the colour encodes a metric; in Timeline the regions appear in the order they were established.

Modes shown: interactive hotspots, choropleth (data-driven colour), animated border draw-on, staggered reveal, establishment timeline, guided zoom tour, pseudo-3D elevation tilt, and a density heat-glow. Every one is pure SVG/CSS in a single file — no libraries, no external assets — so it folds straight into your self-contained AVA tool. Reduced-motion users get instant states instead of animation.
