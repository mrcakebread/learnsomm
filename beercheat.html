Beer Styles
Lager / PilsnerStart Here▼
ProfileCrisp, clean, refreshing
NotesFresh bread, crackers, light grass, subtle floral hops
ExamplesPilsner Urquell, Stella Artois, Modelo
Pairs withPizza, fried foods, light salads
Best forThe most universally drinkable category — a safe order anywhere
Wheat Beer▼
ProfileSweet, creamy, often spiced by the yeast itself
NotesBanana, clove, vanilla, soft dough
ExamplesWeihenstephaner Hefeweizen, Allagash White
Pairs withBrunch, light seafood, fruit salads
Heads upThe banana/clove notes come from the yeast strain, not added flavoring — totally natural and a hallmark of the style.
Pale Ale▼
ProfileBalanced, hoppy without overwhelming
NotesGrapefruit, citrus peel, pine, light floral
ExamplesSierra Nevada Pale Ale, Fuller's London Pride
Pairs withBurgers, roast chicken, sharp cheeses
Best forThe natural next step from a Lager — a gentle introduction to hop character
IPA (India Pale Ale)Bold▼
ProfileBold, intense, aromatic — hops take center stage
NotesMango, resin, orange peel, grapefruit
ExamplesStone IPA, Lagunitas IPA, Bell's Two Hearted
Pairs withSpicy curry, blue cheese, grilled meats
Heads up"West Coast" IPAs are drier and more bitter; "Hazy/New England" IPAs are softer, juicier, and less bitter despite looking similar in ABV.
Amber Ale▼
ProfileToasted, malty, sweet
NotesCaramel, toffee, biscuit, roasted nuts
ExamplesFat Tire, New Belgium Amber, Killian's Irish Red
Pairs withBBQ, roasted vegetables, mild cheddar
Best forMalt-lovers who want flavor without much bitterness
Stout / Porter▼
ProfileRoasted, rich, smooth
NotesCoffee, dark chocolate, dark fruit, sometimes smoke
ExamplesGuinness, Founders Porter, Left Hand Milk Stout
Pairs withOysters, chocolate desserts, smoked meats
Heads upDespite the dark color and rich flavor, most stouts are lower in ABV than people expect — Guinness is only about 4.2%.
Beer Cocktails & Usage
Michelada▼
MixLager, lime juice, hot sauce, Worcestershire, salted rim
StyleSavory · Spicy · Refreshing
Order tipA Mexican brunch staple — order it if you'd enjoy a Bloody Mary but with beer instead of vodka.
Shandy / Radler▼
MixLager or wheat beer, lemonade or citrus soda
StyleLight · Fruity · Low-ABV
Order tipA great hot-weather, low-alcohol option — common at European beer gardens.
Black and Tan▼
MixPale ale (bottom), stout floated on top
StyleLayered · Rich · Balanced
Order tipThe float relies on density differences — a skilled bartender pours slowly over a spoon to keep the layers distinct.
Snakebite▼
MixLager, hard cider (roughly equal parts)
StyleCrisp · Tart · Effervescent
Order tipPopular in UK pubs — a good option if you like beer but want something a bit lighter and tarter.
Beer Float▼
MixStout or porter, vanilla bean ice cream
StyleDessert · Creamy · Roasted
Order tipTreat it like an adult root beer float — the roasted malt notes pair surprisingly well with vanilla ice cream.
