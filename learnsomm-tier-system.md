# LearnSomm Tier System — Wheels, Cheat Sheets & Pricing
*Canon spec. Build every wheel and cheat sheet against this. Curated by Mark Lenington · Certified Sommelier.*

## The core idea: two independent axes
- **Wheel tier = BREADTH** (how many items appear on the wheel).
- **Cheat tier = DEPTH** (how much is said about each item).
They're separate on purpose, so a learner can mix them and price flexes across combos.

Tier names (use these everywhere): **Intro · Next Level · Master**
(= your "intro / mid / max"; mirrors the CMS Intro→Certified→Master ladder.)

| Tier | Who it's for | One-line promise |
|---|---|---|
| Intro | "I don't want to feel lost." | Recognize and talk about the famous few. |
| Next Level | Floor / bar / line working knowledge. | Be genuinely useful to a guest or a cook. |
| Master | Exam-grade geek. | Know the rules, the chemistry, the edge cases. |

## Wheel breadth — inclusion test per tier
- **Intro (3–7 items):** "You'd see this on any list/menu." The canonical names only.
- **Next Level (12–20 items):** "If you work in F&B, you need this." The working set + the main styles/sub-types.
- **Master (30–50+ items):** "Everything, including what wins arguments." Regional, obscure, regulatory, grower-level.

**Digestibility rule:** if a beginner needs a second definition to understand the first, it belongs in a higher tier. Jargon is a Next-Level privilege.

### Worked example — SPARKLING WINE
- **Intro:** Champagne · Prosecco · Cava · Sparkling Rosé · American sparkling
- **Next Level:** + Crémant (as a group) · Franciacorta · Lambrusco · Sekt · Moscato d'Asti · Pét-Nat · English sparkling · Champagne styles (Brut / Extra Brut / Blanc de Blancs / Blanc de Noirs / Rosé)
- **Master:** + every Crémant by region (Alsace, Bourgogne, Loire, Limoux, Jura, Die, Savoie, Bordeaux) · grower vs house Champagne · vintage vs NV · Trentodoc · Cap Classique · Sekt b.A. · Espumante · dosage levels (Brut Nature→Doux) · disgorgement/lees logic

### Worked example — CHEESE (food parallel)
- **Intro:** Cheddar · Mozzarella · Brie · Parmesan · Swiss
- **Next Level:** + Gouda · Blue · Goat (chèvre) · Feta · Manchego · Gruyère · fresh vs aged · rind types (bloomy/washed/natural)
- **Master:** + specific AOP/DOP (Comté, Roquefort, Parmigiano-Reggiano vintages) · raw vs pasteurized · affinage stages · washed-rind microbiology · milk type/breed · terroir

## Cheat-sheet depth — stacked info layers
Each item has stackable layers. **Intro shows 1–4. Next Level shows 1–8. Master shows all.**

### Beverage cheat layers
1. What it is, one plain sentence *(Intro)*
2. Where it's from — country/region, plain *(Intro)*
3. Sweet or dry + one "tastes like" word *(Intro)*
4. Price band ($/$$/$$$) + the occasion you'd drink it *(Intro)*
5. Production method in plain terms + key grapes/ingredients *(Next Level)*
6. Sweetness/strength scale + serving temp + glassware *(Next Level)*
7. 2–3 real pairings + a "say this to a guest" line *(Next Level)*
8. Main styles / sub-types *(Next Level)*
9. Appellation & aging rules + blend % *(Master)*
10. Production chemistry/process (autolysis, atmospheres, distillation) *(Master)*
11. Notable producers + vintage logic + exam tasting markers *(Master)*
12. Faults, history, trivia — full geek *(Master)*

### Food cheat layers
1. What it is *(Intro)*
2. What part / category it is *(Intro)*
3. Flavor + texture in plain words *(Intro)*
4. How it's usually cooked/served + one classic pairing *(Intro)*
5. Cooking methods that work + handling/doneness *(Next Level)*
6. Flavor affinities + seasoning logic *(Next Level)*
7. **Beverage pairing (cross-link to bev wheels)** + substitutions *(Next Level)*
8. Cuts / varieties / grades *(Next Level)*
9. Sourcing / breed / variety / grade & AOC rules *(Master)*
10. Composition/chemistry (fat, collagen, umami, ripening/affinage) *(Master)*
11. Classic preparations by cuisine + technique detail *(Master)*
12. Seasonality, provenance, sustainability, history *(Master)*

## Pricing — a grid, not a line
Price the wheel tier and the cheat tier independently, then reward bundling.

**Base multipliers (set your own base $B):**
| Component | Intro | Next Level | Master |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wheel | 1× B | 2× B | 3× B |
| Cheat sheet | 0.6× B | 1.2× B | 2× B |

**Combos:**
- **À la carte:** any single wheel or cheat at its tier price. Tier-mixing allowed (price just sums).
- **Matched Set** (same-category wheel + cheat, any tiers): −15%.
- **Category Complete** (all 3 wheel tiers + all 3 cheat tiers + that category's quizzes): premium pack, ~−25% vs buying all six.
- **All-Access** (everything + Lightning + Quest + future categories): subscription, top price.

Free path stays: the Learning Quest unlocks Intro-level access by studying, so nobody is paywalled out of starting.

## Build-Your-Own-Challenge toggles
- **Cheat sheets: ON / OFF** — ON = open-book (gentler, fewer points). OFF = closed-book (harder, more points).
- **Cheat tier: Intro / Next Level / Master** (only if ON).
- **Wheel tier: Intro / Next Level / Master** (which item set the challenge draws from).

**Difficulty & points scale together:**
- Open-book + Intro = on-ramp (lowest points).
- Closed-book + Master = exam simulation (highest points).
- Points awarded on **pass at 85%**; 1 point per pass (Lightning Hour is the separate, tiered board: 8/10=1, 9/10=2, 10/10=3).

## Bilingual access & pricing (EN / ES)
Two distinct things, priced separately:
1. **Bilingual *presentation*** — the EN/ES toggle on existing wheels/sheets (same content, two languages).
2. **Bilingual *training program*** — additional Spanish-first wheels & sheets (learn the trade in Spanish, not just translate it). This is its own catalog of content, priced like any wheel/sheet/bundle.

**Who gets bilingual presentation:**
- **Subscribers** — included, full access, all content.
- **Any 90-day challenge** — included for the duration.
- **Permanent (à la carte) buyers** — *not* included by default. They can **add Spanish** as a one-time fee, **calculated per item** (this sheet / this wheel / this bundle / this challenge). 

**The global-language rule:** once a permanent buyer turns Spanish on for *any* item, the EN/ES presentation applies across **everything they own** — they don't re-buy language per item after the first unlock. (The per-item "calculated fee" is how the add-on is *priced/surfaced*; the entitlement, once bought, is global.)

**Calculated bilingual fee — framework (set your own base):**
| Add bilingual to… | Suggested fee |
|---|---|
| Single cheat sheet | + small flat (e.g. 0.3× B) |
| Single wheel | + 0.4× B |
| Category bundle | + 0.6× B (covers all items in it) |
| 90-day challenge | included (no add-on) |
| Subscription | included (no add-on) |

**Gate behavior in the product (built into the wheel):**
- Entitled (subscriber / challenge / bought add-on): EN/ES toggle works everywhere.
- Not entitled: tapping ES opens a bilingual upsell (presentation fee + a nod to the bilingual training program), and the wheel stays in English.
- Driven by a `bilingual` entitlement flag (URL `?subscriber=1` / `?challenge=1` / `?bilingual=1`, or dashboard `LS_CMD {cmd:'bilingual', value:true}`).

## Vault bundles (Intro / Next Level / Master)
The **Vault** is the all-access bundle for a tier: **every cheat sheet, every food & beverage program, and every lesson at that tier's depth.**

- **Intro Vault** — all Intro-tier wheels, cheats, and lessons. Priced as a deliberate **on-ramp**: proportionate to the "digestible progression" principle, low enough to be an easy yes, high enough to value the user's time. The win-win entry point — the user gets a complete, coherent beginner education; the business gets a wide top-of-funnel and a natural climb toward Next Level.
- **Next Level Vault** — everything at Intro + Next Level depth.
- **Master Vault** — the entire catalog, all tiers, all depth. The complete professional reference.

**Suggested pricing shape (set base $B):** Intro Vault ≈ a friendly multiple of a single wheel (cheap relative to buying pieces — that's the point); Next Level Vault a clear step up; Master Vault the premium all-access. Each tier's Vault should cost meaningfully less than buying its contents à la carte (the bundle discount is the incentive). Keep the Intro→Next→Master jumps proportionate to the added breadth + depth so each upgrade feels fair.

**Entitlements that come with a Vault bundle:** owning any Vault grants **clean (no-paywall) wheels** for that tier, **bilingual presentation**, and **Pairing Bench** access. (Vault rank 1/2/3 = Intro/Next/Master.)

## Pairing Bench — gated
Pairing mode (beverage + food side by side) is a **paid capability**, available only to:
- **Subscribers**, **90-day challenge** participants, or **any Vault bundle** owner.

Free/à-la-carte users who tap Pairing get an upsell and are pointed at the bundles. (Flag: `window.LSEnt.canPair()` — URL `?subscriber=1` / `?challenge=1` / `?vault=intro|next|master`, or the stored equivalents.)

## Lightning Hour — dual track + 3-strikes
**Two quizzes every hour, on the hour — one Beverage, one Food.** 24 hours × 2 = up to **48 rounds/day**. Rationale: recovering alcoholics (and anyone off alcohol) get a full **food** path; beverage-only learners get theirs. **We guarantee learning every hour for both food and beverage.** Each track is its own shared, deterministic, play-once-per-hour round, point-weighted by score, integrated with My Journey progress.

**3-strikes mastery rule (BUILT):** the same Lightning question never appears more than **3 times** for a user. Once a term (e.g. classifying a sweet potato) has been shown 3 times, it's retired from that user's rotation forever. Tracked per-term in localStorage (`ls_lh_seen:<term>`); buildQs excludes any term at count ≥3, falling back to the full pool only if a theme is fully mastered so the hour still runs.

## Reinforcement quizzing (spaced repetition) — separate from Lightning
Even when a user **tests out perfectly**, knowledge fades. So every passed topic enters a **spaced-repetition reinforcement schedule** so it doesn't fall off:

- **Check 1:** 2 weeks after testing out.
- **Check 2:** 1 month after Check 1.
- **Check 3:** 3 months after Check 2.
- **…and so on**, each interval longer than the last (expanding schedule). Knowledge stays unless, as Mark puts it, dementia intervenes.

This is **not** Lightning. Lightning is the hourly drop-in game; reinforcement is the user's personal long-term retention track, scheduled per topic from the date they passed. Implementation contract: on a pass, store `{topic, passedAt, nextReviewAt, stage}`; each day surface any topic whose `nextReviewAt` is due; on a passed review, advance the stage and push `nextReviewAt` out by the next interval (2w → 1m → 3m → 6m → …). Failing a review pulls the interval back in.

**Lightning quarterly reset (BUILT):** independent of the above — every ~90 days, the Lightning 3-strikes counters wipe so all questions reopen for everyone. With a large enough question bank there shouldn't be boring stretches before the reset anyway. (Stamp `ls_lh_reset`; on load, if ≥90 days elapsed, clear all `ls_lh_seen:*`.)

## Two parallel tracks: Permanent vs 90-Day Challenge
A user can run **both at once**, and they do **not** overlap:

- **Permanent track** — what you own forever (à-la-carte purchases, Vault bundles). Its quizzing/testing and the reinforcement schedule above are yours permanently.
- **90-Day Challenge track** — its **own unique track**. Challenge **features and access last exactly 90 days**, then you're returned to the normal (permanent) quiz track. The challenge has **reinforcement quizzing more tightly locked in** than Lightning — it's a structured 90-day push, not a casual drop-in.

Key rules:
- Starting a challenge does **not** merge with or overwrite permanent progress; it's a separate progress lane.
- Unlocking something **permanently** does not mean its quizzing/testing overlaps the challenge's — they're tracked independently.
- The user must be **clearly told** challenge access is for 90 days only, after which challenge-only features lock and they continue on the normal track (keeping whatever they unlocked permanently).
- Both tracks can be active simultaneously, each with its own schedule and progress display in My Journey.

## Upgrades, credit & the points economy
Nobody re-buys what they already own. Owning a lower tier always **credits** toward the higher one — you pay only the **difference**, or you **test out** of it for free.

**Two ways to climb (per product, per bundle, per category):**
1. **Test out** — pass the wheel/quiz at the next tier using the cheat sheet you already own. Free. (Ties into the test-out engine.)
2. **Pay the difference** — upgrade price = (target tier price − what you already paid), never full price again.

**Anchors (sake, 30-day solo — Mark's numbers):**
- Intro sake → Next-Level sake: **$10**
- Intro sake → all sake (full category/Master): **$22**
- (Implied) Next-Level sake → Master: **$12**, so Intro→Master in two steps still totals $22.

These live in `window.LSUpgrade` (`UPGRADE_DELTA`); the same pay-the-difference logic applies **everywhere** — every product, every tier jump, solo or category. When the real base price `$B` is set, override the deltas to match.

**Points system (built — `window.LSPoints`):**
- One ledger, one balance (`ls_points`), full history (`ls_points_log`). Quizzes, Lightning, and arcades all earn into it.
- Points have a **dollar value** (placeholder **100 pts = $1**, `setRate()` to change) so they stay proportionate to money.
- Points can be **applied to anything**: upgrades, product/category/bundle purchases — not just "brownie points." `LSUpgrade.applyPoints(from,to)` spends what you have toward an upgrade and returns the remaining dollars owed.
- This keeps the whole economy proportionate: earning is worth real progress, and a motivated free climber can grind points toward a paid tier instead of paying cash.

**What's built vs pending:** the ledger, the dollar-value rate, and the pay-the-difference math are live and verified. The actual *charging* of the remaining difference, and *test-out-to-upgrade*, land with the accounts/checkout + test-out engine. The numbers and rules are locked; only the payment rails remain.

## Personalization: Trim & the Sharpen Wheel
Two ways a paying/unlocked user makes a wheel their own.

**Trim (built).** On any wheel they've unlocked, a "✂ Trim" button (entitled-only) lets them tap descriptors to hide ones they've mastered or don't care about. Hidden items drop from the wheel, the spin pool, and the cheat-sheet list — and stay gone across sessions (`ls_trim:<wheel>`). "Restore all" brings them back. The list honors the same hidden set, so trimming the wheel trims the list automatically.

**Sharpen Wheel (foundation built; full build pending the test-out engine).** Rather than make pass holders hand-build a custom wheel from scratch, the system builds it *for* them from their performance gaps. Every wrong answer in a quiz/Lightning round records a miss (`window.LSGap.miss(term)`); two misses qualifies a term as a weak spot; a correct answer eases it back off. The Sharpen Wheel auto-populates from `LSGap.items()` — so "this person keeps blowing the radish question" → radish lands on their Sharpen Wheel automatically. The list has the same thing: a "Sharpen list" of just your weak spots.

*Naming:* "gap wheel" sounds like a deficiency report — too negative for a learning tool. Recommended name: **Sharpen Wheel** (sharpen your palate) — encouraging, on-brand. Alternatives if you prefer: **Focus Wheel**, **Tune-Up Wheel**, **Polish**, or **Blind Spots** (if you want it blunt). Used "Sharpen" in the spec; easy to swap.

*Status:* the trim feature is live and verified. The Sharpen store records misses and is wired into Lightning now; the auto-built wheel UI and a manual build-your-own picker light up alongside the full quiz/test-out engine (which produces most of the performance data).

## Inclusions & separate-tier gating
Buying a wheel doesn't just unlock that wheel — it includes a set of free companion wheels. But tiers are **separate ratings**: a higher purchase does NOT cascade-unlock everything beneath it. (`window.LSInclusions`.)

**Free entry tier — included with ANY paid purchase:** Coffee, Tea, Mocktails (plus Bread & Water). You don't pay for these once you've bought anything; you can still earn points/brownie points on them by passing their quizzes & tests (access and scoring are independent).

**Per-purchase inclusions (examples, configurable):**
- Buy **Tequila** → also get Coffee, Tea, Mocktails free. Other spirits (Gin, Rum…) stay locked.
- Buy **Wine + Beer** → also get Cocktails, Tea, Mocktails free.

**The Spirits mid-tier is separate.** Buying the fermented-beverage tier (Wine, Beer) does **not** unlock Tequila, Gin, Rum, Vodka, Scotch, Whiskey, Brandy, Cognac, Cordials. Those are a distinct rating with their own value — to get them you either **buy them** or **earn them by quizzing/testing up from the bottom, adjacently**. This keeps the spirits track meaningful instead of a freebie.

**Earning is never gated by how you got the wheel.** Whether a wheel was paid for or included free, passing its quizzes/tests earns points the same way (`LSInclusions.canEarn` follows access, not purchase).

`LSInclusions.access(topic)` returns `'paid'` / `'included'` / `false`. The inclusion map and the spirits list are single config blocks — easy to retune as the rating structure firms up. The gating UI (lock badges, "buy/earn to unlock" prompts) wires onto this with checkout; the rules and the logic are done and verified.

## Multilingual schema + Admin console (built — admin.html)
The headache-saver, done before the big wheel build so adding a language is just filling slots.

**Canonical schema.** Every user-facing string is a keyed entry with language slots: `en` (base, required), `es` (required for launch), `it`/`de`/`fr` (optional, fall back to EN at runtime). Runtime helper: `t(v,lang)` returns `v[lang] || v.en || firstNonEmpty`. Plain legacy strings still work. Wheels/cheats/UI datasets should adopt this shape now.

**Admin console (`admin.html`)** — self-contained, LearnSomm-themed, light/dark:
- **Edit strings:** a table of every entry, editable per language (EN/ES/IT/DE/FR), with key + context, per-row fill dots, live coverage % per language, and search. Every edit is logged automatically with the editor's name.
- **Triple-checker** — three gates, must all pass to publish:
  1. *Structural* — every entry has a key + EN base, no duplicates, valid parse.
  2. *Language completeness & suitability* — required EN+ES present; placeholder/token parity across all filled languages (so `{x}`/`%s` don't drift); optional IT/DE/FR coverage reported (not blocking, falls back to EN).
  3. *Assistant sign-off* — a named reviewer + "reviewed & suitable for all active languages" checkbox.
- **Publish** is disabled until all three gates pass; publishing writes a signed entry to the log.
- **Change log** — every edit and publish (who · when · key·lang · old→new), persisted, exportable.
- **Import/Export JSON** — round-trips the strings table for the build pipeline.

### Admin console — clarified scope (any field, accuracy-first)
The console isn't only for translation — it's the single place to change **any user-facing field**, most often to correct the **accuracy of what's taught**. Two guardrails support that:
- **Reason trail:** every edit carries a free-text reason (e.g. "accuracy fix") into the change log alongside who/when/old→new.
- **Stale-translation detection:** when the base (EN) text is changed, any existing ES/IT/DE/FR for that entry is flagged **stale** (amber dot + a per-row "⚠ recheck" button). The triple-checker's Gate 2 **fails and blocks publishing** while a stale translation exists, so a corrected fact can never go out paired with an out-of-date translation. A reviewer clicks "recheck" (or re-edits the translation) to confirm it's still accurate, which clears the flag and is itself logged.

## Challenge check-ins (built — window.LSCheckins)
When a challenge starts (`chalStart`), it records `ls_chal_active` {start, days, topics, level}. `window.LSCheckins` computes a milestone schedule that scales to the challenge length:
- **Week 1** (day 7) — "One week in — how's it landing?"
- **25%** — "25% there — the habit is forming"
- **Halfway (50%)** — "Halfway — you've built real ground"
- **Final corner (~83%, day 75 of 90)** — "You're most of the way there" → *if you start planning the next round now, this final stretch will feel like a pre-game instead of a finish line. Want to study something else for another stretch?* (the next-round nudge)
- **Final stretch (days-3)** — "Final stretch — bring it home"

Each email body name-drops the user's topics. For a 90-day challenge the days land on 7 / 23 / 45 / 75 / 87; proportional for 30- and 60-day. API: `LSCheckins.schedule()` (full list w/ dates + sent/due state), `.due()` (currently-due), `.markSent(id)`, `.simulateDay(n)` (testing), `.active()`.

These surface **in My Journey** now (a "Challenge check-ins" section: schedule with due-now highlighted and a "Copy email" button per due item, which marks it sent). The Firebase backend will fire these as real scheduled emails later — the schedule + copy are the source of truth it consumes.

### Check-in branching — day-75 response drives day-87
The day-75 "final corner" check-in asks if they want another round (Yes/No buttons on the card; `LSCheckins.respondCorner('yes'|'no')`, stored in `ls_chal_corner_answer`).
- **Declined ('no'):** the day-87 "final" email is **suppressed** (`skip()` true) — don't pitch the next round again; the schedule simply ends after the corner. (Verified: ids end at `corner`.)
- **Accepted ('yes'):** day-87 becomes a **celebratory reward** — subject "Job well done — here's your reward," offering to **upgrade their current unlock to the next tier at a discount** to celebrate finishing and roll into the next stretch.
- **No answer:** neutral default ("the next round is one tap away").

## Category testing ladder (Quest tests — NOT Lightning Hour)
Separate timed test system per category, on the existing Quest engine. Raised bars + timed-to-deter-cheating:
- **Quiz:** 10 Q, **80%** to pass.
- **Medium Test:** 15 Q, **90%**.
- **Final Test:** **30 Q, 90%**.
- All three are **timed per-question** (lightning-style, but not Lightning Hour).

**Speed = points lever** (scout's-honor vs. pushing them). Before each test the user picks pace: **4s (fastest, most points, AI can't realistically cheat) · 8s (fewer) · 15s (least — cheatable with AI+mic so lowest reward)**. Points = base (quiz 100 / medium 180 / final 300) × speed multiplier (4s ×1.0, 8s ×0.6, 15s ×0.3), awarded via `LSPoints.earn` and shown in the pass verdict. Per-question countdown auto-marks wrong + advances on timeout.
