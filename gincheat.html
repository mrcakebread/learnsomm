The Styles
London DryStart Here▼
ProfileClassic, juniper-forward
NotesJuniper, citrus zest, pine, warm spice
ExamplesTanqueray, Beefeater, Bombay Sapphire
Pairs withSmoked salmon, oysters, charcuterie, sharp cheddar
Best forMartinis, G&Ts — the benchmark everything else is measured against
Contemporary / Floral▼
ProfileLighter on juniper, led by floral or citrus botanicals
NotesCucumber, rose, lavender, elderflower, fresh citrus
ExamplesHendrick's (rose/cucumber), Monkey 47, Empress 1908
Pairs withLight salads, sushi, soft cheeses, cucumber dishes
Best forGin skeptics — if you think you don't like gin, try this style first
Old TomSpecialty▼
ProfileSlightly sweeter and maltier than London Dry
NotesEarthy juniper, light vanilla, honey, soft spice
ExamplesHayman's Old Tom, Ransom Old Tom
Pairs withRich appetizers, pâté, aged cheeses
Heads upNot common on grocery shelves — specialty liquor stores or good cocktail bars. Great in a Tom Collins or Martinez.
Navy StrengthHigh ABV▼
ProfileSame as London Dry but bottled at 57% ABV minimum
NotesIntense juniper, bold spice, more heat
ExamplesPerry's Tot, Hayman's Royal Dock, Plymouth Navy Strength
Pairs withSpicy food, bold flavors that can stand up to it
Heads upThat's ~114 proof — nearly double a standard pour's ABV. Not a beginner's first glass neat; excellent in cocktails where you want the gin to cut through.
Sloe GinNot True Gin▼
What it isGin infused with sloe berries and sugar — technically a liqueur
NotesSweet cherry, plum, almond, gentle spice
ExamplesPlymouth Sloe Gin, Gordon's Sloe Gin
Pairs withDesserts, chocolate, cheese boards
Heads upYou'll see it on back bars looking like gin — it's sweeter and lower ABV. Great in a Sloe Gin Fizz. Don't order it expecting a standard gin drink.
Cocktails
Gin & Tonic▼
MixLondon Dry gin, quality tonic water, lime or lemon
StyleCrisp · Bitter · Refreshing
Order tipAsk for a premium tonic (Fever-Tree, Q Tonic) — cheap tonic is the #1 reason a G&T disappoints. The tonic is half the drink.
Martini▼
MixLondon Dry gin, dry vermouth
StyleElegant · Dry · Spirit-forward
Order tipIf you're new to gin, order it "wet" — more vermouth softens the spirit. Most bars default to very dry, which is a lot of gin.
Negroni▼
MixGin, Campari, sweet vermouth (equal parts)
StyleBitter · Balanced · Complex
Order tipOne of the great cocktails. If you find it too bitter at first, ask for a Negroni Sbagliato — same build but Campari + sweet vermouth topped with prosecco instead of gin. Lighter entry point.
French 75▼
MixLondon Dry gin, lemon juice, simple syrup, topped with Champagne
StyleElegant · Bright · Celebratory
Order tipOne of the most elegant gin drinks and widely available. A great "I want gin but something approachable" order at any bar or restaurant.
Gimlet▼
MixGin, fresh lime juice, simple syrup
StyleTart · Crisp · Clean
Order tipAsk for fresh lime, not Rose's lime cordial — the classic recipe used cordial but fresh is significantly better and most good bars will use it.
Tom Collins▼
MixLondon Dry gin, lemon juice, simple syrup, soda water
StyleTall · Effervescent · Easy-drinking
Order tipGreat gateway gin cocktail — the soda and lemon mellow the spirit considerably. Good "I'm not sure about gin yet" order.
Aviation Bar Dependent▼
MixGin, maraschino liqueur, crème de violette, lemon juice
StyleFloral · Vibrant · Violet-purple color
Heads upStunning when made well, but crème de violette is uncommon — many bars skip it and the cocktail loses its character. Ask if they make a "proper Aviation" before ordering.
