The Regions
SpeysideStart Here▼
ProfileSweet, fruity, elegant
NotesHoney, vanilla, apple, dried fruit, sherry
ExamplesGlenlivet, Macallan, Glenfiddich
Pairs withSoft cheeses, dried fruit and nut boards, light desserts
Best forThe easiest entry point into single malts — sets the baseline for "elegant" Scotch
Highland▼
ProfileDiverse, robust, complex — the broadest region
NotesToffee, heather, light spice, malt, occasional light smoke
ExamplesGlenmorangie, Dalmore, Oban
Pairs withRoasted nuts, smoked meats, dark chocolate
Heads upBecause the region is huge, "Highland" tells you less than other regions — flavor varies a lot bottle to bottle.
IslayBold▼
ProfileIntense, smoky, coastal
NotesCampfire smoke, brine, iodine, seaweed, antiseptic
ExamplesLaphroaig, Ardbeg, Lagavulin
Pairs withOysters, smoked salmon, blue cheese — big flavors that won't get bulldozed
Heads upThe most polarizing region. Love-it-or-hate-it smoke level — don't make this your first Scotch.
Lowland▼
ProfileLight, soft, gentle — often triple distilled
NotesGrass, honeysuckle, cream, light citrus
ExamplesAuchentoshan, Glenkinchie
Pairs withLight fish, salads, mild cheeses
Best forGin or vodka drinkers easing into whisky — the softest entry style
Blended Scotch▼
ProfileBalanced, approachable, consistent batch to batch
NotesCaramel, grain sweetness, subtle oak
ExamplesJohnnie Walker, Chivas Regal, Dewar's
Pairs withBurgers, pub food, anything casual
Best forMixing — blends hold their character in cocktails better (and cheaper) than single malts
Cocktails
Rob Roy▼
MixBlended Scotch, sweet vermouth, Angostura bitters
StyleBalanced · Sophisticated · Classic
Order tipBasically a Scotch Manhattan. A safe, classy order at any cocktail bar.
Penicillin▼
MixBlended Scotch, honey-ginger syrup, lemon juice, Islay Scotch float
StyleSmoky · Warming · Spicy
Order tipA modern classic (invented ~2005). The smoky float on top is the signature — don't skip it if offered.
Rusty Nail▼
MixScotch, Drambuie (honeyed Scotch liqueur)
StyleSimple · Warming · Sweet-Herbal
Order tipJust two ingredients, stirred over ice. A great after-dinner sipper.
Blood and Sand▼
MixScotch, Cherry Heering, sweet vermouth, orange juice (equal parts)
StyleFruity · Complex · Balanced
Order tipOne of the few classic cocktails that uses orange juice — surprisingly elegant despite the unusual build.
Scotch Sour▼
MixScotch, lemon juice, simple syrup, egg white (optional)
StyleBright · Tangy · Creamy
Order tipAsk for it "with egg white" for the classic frothy texture — totally fine to skip if you're squeamish, the drink works either way.
