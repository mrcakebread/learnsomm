The Styles
White / Silver RumStart Here▼
AgeUnaged, or aged then charcoal-filtered for clarity
NotesSugarcane, light citrus, coconut, subtle vanilla
ExamplesBacardi Superior, Plantation 3 Star, Cruzan Light
Pairs withCeviche, grilled shrimp, tropical fruit
Best forMixing — the cleanest canvas for citrus-forward cocktails
Gold / Amber Rum▼
AgeLightly aged in oak, typically 1–4 years
NotesVanilla, caramel, toasted almond, light oak
ExamplesMount Gay Eclipse, Appleton Estate Signature, Bacardi Gold
Pairs withGrilled pork, baked plantain, pecan desserts
Best forA step up from white rum — sippable on its own or in slightly richer cocktails
Dark RumBold▼
AgeLonger aging, often 5+ years, sometimes blended with rich molasses-based rums
NotesMolasses, baking spice, dark chocolate, dried fruit
ExamplesGosling's Black Seal, Myers's Original Dark, Plantation Original Dark
Pairs withBBQ, gingerbread, dark chocolate desserts
Heads upOften the most intensely flavored category — a little goes a long way in cocktails, which is why it's usually "floated" rather than fully mixed.
Spiced RumSpecialty▼
AgeVaries — base rum infused with spices after aging
NotesCinnamon, nutmeg, vanilla, clove, sometimes vanilla bean
ExamplesCaptain Morgan, Sailor Jerry, Kraken
Pairs withSpiced cake, apple desserts, holiday cooking
Heads upCloser to a flavored liqueur than a "pure" rum category — great with cola, less great in classic rum cocktails that expect a clean base.
Cocktails
Daiquiri▼
MixWhite rum, fresh lime juice, simple syrup
StyleCrisp · Refreshing · Balanced
Order tipThe "real" Daiquiri is just these three ingredients, shaken — not the frozen, fruity slushy version. Ask for it "classic" if you want the original.
Mojito▼
MixWhite rum, mint, lime, sugar, soda water
StyleHerby · Effervescent · Lively
Order tipThe mint should be gently pressed, not pulverized — over-muddled mint turns bitter and grassy.
Dark 'n' Stormy▼
MixDark rum (floated), ginger beer, lime juice
StyleSpicy · Deep · Robust
Order tipThe dark rum should sit on top, not be stirred in — that visual "storm cloud" layering is part of the drink.
Mai Tai▼
MixRum blend (white + dark), orgeat, lime juice, orange liqueur
StyleTropical · Complex · Nutty
Order tipOrgeat (almond syrup) is the key ingredient that separates a real Mai Tai from a sugary imitation — a good bar will have it on hand.
Piña Colada▼
MixWhite or gold rum, pineapple juice, cream of coconut
StyleCreamy · Sweet · Indulgent
Order tipA gold rum float on top adds depth without changing the creamy base — ask for it if you want a slightly more grown-up version.
